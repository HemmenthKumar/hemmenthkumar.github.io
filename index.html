Why I Build.

I never had a mentor as a kid, so I learned by breaking things. Phones, toys, electronics, none were spared; and I put them back together. Watched space and science documentaries my whole life to know how everything worked. That curiosity, from the cosmos to circuits, is now my compass.

Today, I build Idea Laboratory so others don’t have to learn alone.

“This isn’t about degrees. It’s about turning raw curiosity into real things.”

So Why Idea Laboratory?

The Indian government has set up thousands of AICTE-IDEA Labs across the country. On paper, they’re meant to turn student ideas into real startups. But the reality is harsh: only 217 startups were formally registered from over 12,000 ideas (AICTE Annual Report, 2022–23).

Why? Because labs are handed over to overloaded faculty volunteering their time. With no one to help with dedicated prototyping, equipment sits unused because no one knows how to operate it. Students are left to learn CAD, electronics, design, and everything else alone—usually meaning they must already be capable enough to create a startup, or they give up, even if their idea was viable.

They move on. Idea Laboratory exists to stop that leak.

What I Do.

I help non-engineer innovators; students, founders, dreamers, turn their ideas into functional, investor ready prototypes with full documentation

My process: fundamental understanding of the idea’s existence → technical spec → prototype build → pitch package. I document every step so anyone can replicate it.

My Path Forward.

Stage 1 Build portfolio via Riidl, internships, and freelance R&D consulting. Fund my rTMS treatment. Document everything.

Stage 2 Partner with universities and incubators as a “portable prototyping lab” for student teams.

Stage 3 Secure startup funding and launch a company. Scientists and researchers work in a lab, ideators work in an Idea Laboratory.

Who I Serve.

• Students with research ideas but no prototyping skills

• Pre-seed founders needing investor-ready hardware

• NGOs seeking assistive or community-focused tech

• Universities wanting to scale student innovation capacity
